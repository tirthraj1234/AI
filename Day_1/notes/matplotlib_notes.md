# Matplotlib Notes

## Day 1 – Matplotlib Fundamentals

### What is Matplotlib?

Matplotlib is a Python library used for creating graphs, charts, and visualizations. It helps us represent data visually, making it easier to understand patterns, trends, and relationships. It is one of the most popular visualization libraries used in Data Science and Machine Learning.

---

## Why is Data Visualization Important?

Data visualization helps us:

- Understand data easily.
- Identify patterns and trends.
- Detect outliers or unusual values.
- Compare different datasets.
- Present data in a clear and attractive way.
- Make better business decisions.
- Analyze data before building Machine Learning models.

Visualization is an important step in Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA).

---

## Types of Charts Learned

### 1. Line Chart

A line chart displays data points connected by lines.

**Used for:**
- Showing trends over time.
- Monthly sales analysis.
- Stock market prices.
- Temperature changes.

Example:
- Monthly sales from January to June.

---

### 2. Bar Chart

A bar chart compares values between different categories.

**Used for:**
- Comparing products.
- Comparing employee performance.
- Comparing sales across different departments.
- Comparing student marks.

Example:
- Number of students learning different programming languages.

---

### 3. Pie Chart

A pie chart shows how each category contributes to the whole as a percentage.

**Used for:**
- Market share analysis.
- Budget distribution.
- Department-wise employee distribution.
- Product category sales percentage.

Example:
- Percentage of students learning different programming languages.

---

### 4. Histogram

A histogram shows the distribution of numerical data by grouping values into ranges (bins).

**Used for:**
- Exam marks distribution.
- Employee salary distribution.
- Customer age distribution.
- Product price distribution.

Example:
- Distribution of students' marks.

---

### 5. Scatter Plot

A scatter plot shows the relationship between two numerical variables.

**Used for:**
- Experience vs Salary.
- Height vs Weight.
- Advertising cost vs Sales.
- Study hours vs Exam marks.

Example:
- Relationship between employee experience and salary.

---

## Matplotlib Functions Used

- `plt.plot()` – Create a line chart.
- `plt.bar()` – Create a bar chart.
- `plt.pie()` – Create a pie chart.
- `plt.hist()` – Create a histogram.
- `plt.scatter()` – Create a scatter plot.
- `plt.title()` – Add a title to the graph.
- `plt.xlabel()` – Add a label to the X-axis.
- `plt.ylabel()` – Add a label to the Y-axis.
- `plt.grid()` – Display grid lines.
- `plt.savefig()` – Save the graph as an image.
- `plt.show()` – Display the graph.
